2. Niagra Region

There are many amazing activities and well-known tourist destinations in the Niagara region. In addition to its natural wonder, the area is well-known around the world for the magnificent Niagara Falls. Located near the U.S. border, Niagara offers a unique combination of historical sites, natural beauty, and a thriving local culture. Travellers of all stripes can find something to like at Niagara-on-the-Lake, its picturesque wineries, hiking trails, and the roaring Falls. Whether you're a history buff touring the sites of the War of 1812 or an adrenaline junkie seeking a boat excursion that brings you up close to the Falls, Niagara is unquestionably a must-see while travelling to Ontario. 1812 battles. The area's accessibility from both Canada and the U.S. makes it a convenient yet awe-inspiring spot to explore, creating an unforgettable experience for all who visit.

3. CN Tower

The CN Tower is one of Canada's most recognizable landmarks, towering at 553 meters and offering stunning views of Toronto and the surrounding areas. As one of the tallest freestanding structures in the world, it gives visitors a unique bird’s-eye view of the city and Lake Ontario. Inside the tower, there are several thrilling attractions, like the Glass Floor, where you can look straight down at the city below, and the SkyPod, which features one of the highest observation decks open to the public. But the CN Tower isn’t the only highlight in this part of Toronto. Situated in the heart of downtown, it's just steps away from lively spots like the Entertainment District, known for its top-notch theaters, restaurants, and nightlife. A short walk also brings you to the waterfront, where you can enjoy peaceful lake views, or to popular attractions like the Royal Ontario Museum, Ripley's Aquarium, and the Rogers Centre. Whether you're dining at the 360 Restaurant with a view or discovering the arts, culture, and entertainment all around, this area has something for everyone to enjoy.
